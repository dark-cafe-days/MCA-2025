For my Creative Commons attribution, I chose 'CC BY-NC SA 4.0', or: the "Attribution-NonCommercial-Sharealike" attribution from the most recent set of Creative Commons guidelines. I chose this on the basis that it is the license that gives the best balance of rights between me as the creator of this .mei file and its potential users. By enforcing both the non-commercal and ShareAlike prohibitions, I am attempting to ensure that the .mei file is only used in ways that do not breach copyright law, and to ensure that no-one simply attempts to sell my work for a profit, as the ShareAlike restriction mandates that anyone's copy or remix of my .mei file must also be shared under CC BY-NC SA 4.0.


For my genre classification, I chose to cast as wide a net as possible. 'Genres include Videogame Soundtrack, BGM' does not particularly serve to describe the acoustic qualities of the work, but rather the contexts in which it is typically heard - 'Movie Soundtrack' is still arguably a valid genre category, after all. I chose this because, unlike some of Hosoe's other contributions to the Zero Escape series, Nostalgic Scenery does not quite fit into either the more engergetic, indsutrial style tracks used throughout the puzzle-room segments, nor the more classical and orchestral style found in many of the other non-gameplay moments. Its synthesised melody and gentler feel make it hard to place within the context of the rest of the game's soundtrack.
