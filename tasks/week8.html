The time-frequency analysis' benefits are especially obvious in the spectrogram for the first track, Tick Down. The song (accompanying the game's opening title screen) has the sound of a clock ticking throughout, with an additional melody joining in after 4 seconds. This is arguably visible in the waveform, but is made downright obvious in the spectrogram. There is a more accurate and intuitive sense of not only the timing of the ticks than the waveform gives, but also a better understanding of how the melody ebbs and flows throughout the song's short runtime. Similarly, in Blue Bird Lamentation, the opening few bars of the track are presented as an in-universe music box, before the non-diagetic orchestra swoops in for the bulk of the song. The spectrogram makes it very obvious that this is a case of additional instruments joining the frey for the middle of the song, rather than a simple increase in volume.
